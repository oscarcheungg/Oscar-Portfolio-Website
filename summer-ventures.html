LASSO Regression Model

A statistical model leveraging LASSO regression to identify key ozone to melanoma correlations in New Zealand, developed as part of the Summer Ventures research program at UNC Charlotte.

Project Overview

For this project, I built a LASSO regression model analyzing datasets extracted from the New Zealand Cancer Registry to reveal significant ozone and melanoma correlations. Melanoma, a form of skin cancer, is especially prominent in the New Zealand region as they are directly located near where our ozone layer opens up throughout the year. Collaborating with my partner during my time at NCSSM Summer Ventures, I applied statistical modeling to enhance predictive accuracy in determining the number of melanoma cases based off ozone concentrations. This project deepened my understanding of machine learning and data analysis. Our work culminated in a 30+ page research paper presented to scientists, earning the NC Exemplary Research Award.

Main Objective

How can we develop a LASSO regression model to analyze large datasets, uncover key correlations, and enhance predictive accuracy in scientific research?
